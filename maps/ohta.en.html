🎯 Map Overview

Ohta is a terminus station of the Tokyo Kanagawa Express where two lines arrive and depart. All tracks are dead-ended at the platforms, so every arriving train reverses direction right at its platform and departs again. There is no siding and no shunting to worry about — instead, arrivals and departures of both lines converge on a single set of points at the station throat, and your job is to untangle those conflicting routes as smoothly as possible.

  • Tracks: Tracks 1–4 (dead-end terminus, two island platforms)
  • Yukigaya Line … Track 1 / Track 2
  • Tamagawa Line … Track 3 / Track 4
  • Siding: None (all turnarounds happen at the platforms)
  • Trains: All services are 3-car Local trains — no class or length variations

🔧 Route List

A route is defined by a Start Lever and an End Lever. All routes at Ohta are main-line routes, split into two groups: arrival and departure. There are no shunting routes.

Arrival Routes (approach → platform)

Start Lever "1" handles arrivals from the Yukigaya Line, "2" from the Tamagawa Line.

1B1 → BEntry from the Yukigaya Line into Track 2
2B2 → BEntry from the Tamagawa Line into Track 2 (cross-line move)
2C2 → CEntry from the Tamagawa Line into Track 3
2D2 → DEntry from the Tamagawa Line into Track 4

Departure Routes (platform → main line)

Start Levers "11–14" correspond to each platform track. End Lever "E" leads to the Yukigaya Line, "F" to the Tamagawa Line.

RouteStart → EndPurpose
11E11 → EDeparture from Track 1 onto the Yukigaya Line
12E12 → EDeparture from Track 2 onto the Yukigaya Line
12F12 → FDeparture from Track 2 onto the Tamagawa Line (cross-line move)
13F13 → FDeparture from Track 3 onto the Tamagawa Line
14F14 → FDeparture from Track 4 onto the Tamagawa Line
Track 2 is the only track connected to both lines. Track 1 serves the Yukigaya Line only and Tracks 3–4 serve the Tamagawa Line only, but Track 2 can receive from — and depart to — either line.

🔄 Platform Turnaround Flow

Because Ohta is a dead-end terminus, every train follows the same pattern: arrive → reverse at the platform → depart. With no siding moves involved, each train takes just three steps:

  1. Set the arrival route

    Check which line the approaching train is on (Start Lever "1" for the Yukigaya Line, "2" for the Tamagawa Line), then set the arrival route to the platform shown on the departure board.

  2. Set the departure route

    The arriving train becomes its own return service while passengers board. The return service keeps the same train number with a "_1" suffix added (e.g. 2003 → 2003_1). Check its destination line on the departure board, then set the departure route using that track's Start Lever (11–14) and the End Lever for the correct line (E or F).

  3. Press the departure button

    When departure time comes, press that track's departure button to send the train off.

⚠ Morning Rush: turnarounds every ~3 minutes
In the Morning Rush scenario, trains depart barely three minutes after arriving. When you set an arrival route, check the departure board right away for the return service's time and line — it keeps you one step ahead.

🔀 Mastering Track 2 (cross-line services)

Track 2 is shared by both lines. Most trains simply return the way they came, but the Morning Rush includes a service that arrives from the Yukigaya Line and departs onto the Tamagawa Line.

ArrivalArrival routeDepartureDeparture route
2003 (Yukigaya Line → Track 2)1B2003_1 (Track 2 → Tamagawa Line)12F

Always check the destination shown on the departure board — don't set 12E (Yukigaya Line) out of habit. Note that route 12F cuts diagonally across the station throat and conflicts with many other routes, so open it at a moment when nearby arrivals and departures have settled down.

⚠ Common Mistakes

① Forgetting the departure route

Arriving trains do not turn around by themselves. A train can only depart once both the departure route is set and the departure button is pressed. If you focus only on arrivals, the platforms fill up in no time.

② Sending Track 2 trains to the wrong line

Track 2 can depart to either the Yukigaya Line (12E) or the Tamagawa Line (12F). Confirm the destination on the departure board before setting the route.

③ Route conflicts at the station throat

Every arrival and departure of both lines passes through the same set of points at the station entrance. Conflicting routes cannot be open at the same time, so you will sometimes have to wait for one route to release. Prioritize trains that are about to depart, and let arriving trains wait outside the station when needed.
